Understanding Rare Diseases in Children

What is a Rare Disease?

A rare disease is a condition that affects fewer than 1 in 2,000 people. While each disease is rare, collectively they impact millions of children worldwide.

Challenges Faced

Children with rare diseases often face delayed diagnosis, limited treatment options, and a lack of specialized care. This can significantly impact their quality of life and development.

The Importance of Support

Support networks, like KIDS Rare Hellas, play a crucial role in improving the lives of affected children and their families through advocacy, education, and community building.

Our Objectives

Raise Awareness

Increase public understanding of rare diseases and their impact on children and families.

Provide Support

Offer resources, guidance, and a strong community for families affected by rare diseases.

Advocate for Policy Changes

Work towards improved healthcare policies and research funding for rare diseases.

Foster Community

Create a supportive network where families can connect, share experiences, and find strength.
